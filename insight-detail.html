Why Most Transformation Programs Stall After Month Six

Coming Soon • 5 min read

The pattern shows up almost every time — and it's rarely the strategy that was wrong. Transformation programs across industries share a common trajectory: strong momentum in the first few months, followed by a gradual (or sudden) stall around the six-month mark.

The Six-Month Wall

By month six, the initial enthusiasm has worn off. The quick wins have been won. The team is tired. And the hard, structural changes that were always going to be necessary are now staring everyone in the face.

What separates programs that push through from those that stagnate isn't the quality of the strategy document. It's the quality of the execution infrastructure — the governance, the accountability mechanisms, and the honest conversations that most organizations avoid until it's too late.

Why This Happens

  • Scope creep disguised as adaptation. New priorities get layered onto the original mandate without dropping anything.
  • Leadership distraction. The sponsor who was fully engaged at launch has moved on to the next urgent issue.
  • Change fatigue. Teams are being asked to adopt new ways of working while still delivering their day jobs.
  • Misaligned incentives. The compensation system still rewards the old behaviors.

The Way Through

The organizations that succeed have a few things in common. They maintain engagement at the executive level, not just at launch but at the six-month point when it matters most. They treat transformation as an operational discipline, not a strategic project. And they're honest with themselves about what's really holding progress back.

At Sam & Co, we've seen this pattern across every market we work in. The six-month wall is predictable, which means it's also preventable — if you're willing to name it and build the infrastructure to push through it.
